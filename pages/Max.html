MAX VERSTAPPEN

Born in 29-9-1997, Hasselt Belgium.His career started in 2013, and his nationality is Netherlands

ACHIVMENTS

  • Max wins his first World Driver’s Championship title (Yas Marina Circuit, Abu Dhabi), he scored 10 wins, 18 podium finishes, 10 pole positions and six fastest laps. He ended the season with 395.5 points
  • Max led 652 laps - 50.2% of all race laps. More than double the number of laps led by closest rival Lewis Hamilton (297) and more than the other nine drivers who led races this year combined (348)
  • Becomes the first Formula 1 driver to win all three races of a triple-header (French GP, Styrian GP and Austrian GP)
  • Victory in Monaco (Circuit de Monaco, Monaco), Scored his first win in principality and took the championship lead for the first time
  • 1st Place: Abu Dhabi Grand Prix (Yas Marina Circuit, Abu Dhabi). Second win of the 2020 season
  • Pole Position: Abu Dhabi Grand Prix (Yas Marina Circuit, Abu Dhabi). Third career pole position
  • 1st Place: 70th Anniversary Grand Prix (Silverstone, UK). First win of 2020 the season
  • 1st Place: Brazilian Grand Prix (Autódromo José Carlos Pace, São Paulo, Brazil). Third win of the 2019 season
  • Pole Position: Brazilian Grand Prix (Autódromo José Carlos Pace, São Paulo, Brazil). Second career pole position
  • First Pole Position: Hungarian Grand Prix (Hungaroring, Mogyoród, Hungary). First pole position of F1 career
  • 1st Place: German Grand Prix (Hockenheimring, Hockenheim, Germany). Second win of the season
  • 1st Place: Austrian Grand Prix (Red Bull Ring, Spielberg, Austria). First win of the season and first for Honda in 13 years
  • 1st Place: Mexican Grand Prix (Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez, Mexico City, Mexico). Second win of the season
  • 1st Place: Austrian Grand Prix (Red Bull Ring, Spielberg, Austria). First win of the season
  • 1st Place: Mexican Grand Prix (Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez, Mexico City, Mexico). Second win of the year
  • 1st Place: Malaysian Grand Prix (Sapang International Circuit, Selangor, Malaysia). First win of the year
